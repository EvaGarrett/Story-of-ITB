Ecology of ITB

Our campus is full of biodiversity and wild life, here were are going to have a look at the ecology within ITB grounds.

Flora

Our Campus is filled with many plants and trees, as soon as you enter the college you are met with bright green colours of the grass and trees.
The trees that live in ITB campus are all less than 20 years old.
There are drainage ditches surrounding ITB that are more than 200 years old, some which are 2-3 meters deep.
There are relic hedges and treelines in the area which take up 1200ha.
Some of the hedges in the area have been around since 1837.
Some of the tree species that can be found on ITB campus include; Marare, ash, sycamore, elder, elm, and you will also find nettles, fern, ivy and other wildflowers.
All of the flora on our campus provides purpose, nealry all of it provides a habitat for the wildlife around ITB. Some of the trees and hedges provide shelter for both us and the wildlife, all of the plants also produce oxygen which is crutial.

Fauna

There are many great places throught ITB that is great habitats for lots of wildlife.
There are many rabbit holes around ITB campus, which show evidence that there are rabbits living here, the mature trees on campus provide structure for burrows.
The ivy that is on campus produces berries in spring, this provides food for animals such as bees and wasps so this will attract these to be around the campus.
All of the deep ditches create great habitats for wildlife to live, such as many differnt incects and mice.
There are always many differnt birds also seen around campus, they can feet off the worms in the ground and the berries on the hedges, magpies are a specific that are always seen around the enterence of campus.
